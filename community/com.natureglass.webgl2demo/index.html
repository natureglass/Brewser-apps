WebGL2 Sunset Sea — Procedural ShaderToy Cube

Raw WebGL 2 (GLSL ES 3.00) rendered through the nx.js bridge via #pragma raw_passthrough — no Three.js. A single fullscreen triangle drives a procedural sunset sky (sun, halo, clouds, stars) over an FBM-displaced sea with sun glitter, a chrome cube wearing the nx.js logo that floats on the waves, and a soft shadow + reflection streak that the cube projects onto the water. Touch the screen and drag to look around.


A single triangle covers the screen with gl_VertexID + a const array (no vertex buffer); the fragment shader does the rest: procedural sky with sun + halo, FBM clouds, FBM-displaced sea raymarched in 5 steps, ray-box intersection for the cube, soft cube-on-sea shadow, and a reflection streak that samples the logo texture. CPU computes the cube's float-pose once per frame (yaw + wave roll + wave pitch) and hands it to the shader as uCubeCenter, uCubeR, uCubeInvR. Drag the screen with one finger to orbit the camera. Adaptive resolution from the host version is stripped — the inline canvas is fixed at 640×360 (1280×720 in fullscreen mode).
