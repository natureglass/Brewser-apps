nx.js WebGL Demo — Ocean Cube

Raw WebGL 1 rendered through the nx.js bridge — no Three.js. Procedural night sky with stars + planes, a billboard moon, a 220×220-quad animated ocean with caustic foam, and a chrome cube wearing the nx.js logo that rides the waves. All four shader programs run on native GLES via #pragma raw_passthrough. Right stick orbits the camera; left stick (push up/down) zooms in/out. Touch the screen and drag to orbit as well.


This demo exercises raw WebGL 1 with four custom shader programs (background sky, billboard moon, animated water grid, and a textured chrome cube). All shaders are opt-ed into the bridge's raw-shader passthrough path (#pragma raw_passthrough) so the user GLSL runs end-to-end on native GLES. Right stick orbits the camera; push the left stick up/down to zoom in/out. Single-finger touch + drag also orbits the camera. The cube wears the nx.js logo PNG.
