Three.js r162 — Sprites

Port of three-r162/examples/webgl_sprites.html: 200 THREE.Sprite particles orbiting on a sphere (lit by linear Fog) plus 5 HUD sprites pinned to the screen corners + center rendered with an OrthographicCamera in a second pass (autoClear = false + clearDepth()). First milestone to exercise nxjs-source's new sprite math path: nx_webgl_draw_elements -> draw_sprite_bridge detects center + rotation uniforms (only SpriteMaterial uploads both), extracts world scale from modelMatrix columns, computes screen-aligned quad vertices in view space, then routes through the existing textured-triangle bridge.


Conformance: rendering/gl-drawelements — pins the JS-side validation gates in front of gl.drawElements. Three.js's Sprite uses an indexed quad geometry, so every sprite draw flows through this path.

200 sprites orbit on a sphere of radius 500 around the origin, with per-sprite rotation + scale animation. The orbit is rotated continuously on all three axes. Five HUD sprites are pinned to the canvas corners + center via a second-pass orthographic render. The scene's linear Fog fades distant sprites to black. Known caveats for this initial port: material color tint (material.color.setHSL) won't apply because the bridge's textured fragment shader doesn't currently multiply by u_color; per-sprite opacity animation likewise isn't routed. Sprites will render at full texture color without tinting or opacity fades.
