Three.js r184 — WebGL2 Multiple Render Targets

1:1 port of webgl_multiple_rendertargets.html from three.js latest (r184): a TorusKnotGeometry drawn through a GLSL ES 3.00 RawShaderMaterial into a WebGLRenderTarget({count:2}) G-Buffer (diffuse on layout(location=0), view-space normal on layout(location=1)), then a fullscreen composite pass samples both attachments and outputs mix(diffuse, normal, step(0.5, vUv.x)). Left stick orbits the camera; A cycles MSAA samples (4 → 2 → 1 → 0); X toggles wireframe.


Conformance: rendering/multiple-render-targets — hand-rolled, pins MAX_DRAW_BUFFERS ≥ 2, framebufferTexture2D with COLOR_ATTACHMENT0/1, checkFramebufferStatus COMPLETE for a 2-attachment FBO, drawBuffers accepting both a plain Array and Uint32Array, and a GLSL ES 3.00 layout(location=) out vec4 shader writing 2 distinct outputs in one pass.

MSAA caveat: on Mesa Nouveau (Citron), Three.js's multisampled-RT blitFramebuffer-to-color-textures resolve produces zero in the color textures — default samples = 0 to bypass that path; the A button still cycles up to 4 for hw verification on real Tegra.
