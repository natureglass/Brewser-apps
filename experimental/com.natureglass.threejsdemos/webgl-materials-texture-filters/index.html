Three.js r162 — Texture Filters

Port of webgl_materials_texture_filters.html: two parallel scenes rendered side-by-side via renderer.setScissor. Left half: LinearMipmapLinear on the floor + Linear on the painting. Right half: Nearest everywhere. Both scenes share a 128×128 checkerboard floor tiled 1000× (so it recedes to the horizon and would alias badly without mipmaps) and a Caravaggio Canestra di frutta on a framed plane with a tilted shadow reflection. First milestone to exercise the bridge's mipmap-aware texture filtering — nxjs's texParameteri now accepts the 4 mipmap minFilter variants (NEAREST/LINEAR × MIPMAP × NEAREST/LINEAR), and generateMipmap is no longer a no-op (promotes the texture to a persistent GLES handle via the shared ensure_texture_promoted helper from milestone #22 + calls native glGenerateMipmap).


Conformance: textures/mipmap-filter-and-generate — hand-rolled, pins the milestone-#24 bridge surface: texParameteri accepts the 4 new mipmap MIN_FILTER variants while still rejecting them on MAG_FILTER; generateMipmap actually generates mipmaps on a CPU-data texture (promoting if needed); plus negative cases (undefined texture, CUBE_MAP target, junk enums).

Mouse input from the upstream demo (camera follows cursor) is replaced by gamepad left stick — pan to compare the floor's aliasing characteristics in different positions. The 128×128 floor checkerboard is built procedurally into a DataTexture instead of upstream's CanvasTexture + document.createElement('canvas') because nx.js's texImage2D doesn't accept canvas elements directly. Caravaggio's Canestra di frutta JPG (~149 KB) is loaded via Image + OffscreenCanvas + DataTexture per memory. Visual contrast: the left floor (LinearMipmapLinear) is smooth even at the horizon; the right floor (Nearest) shows the characteristic Moiré / shimmer pattern. The painting contrast is more subtle but visible up close on Citron.
