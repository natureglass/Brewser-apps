Three.js r162 — Morph Targets Sphere

Port of webgl_morphtargets_sphere.html: a 1,876-vertex sphere with two morph targets ("Ship" + "Blob") drawn as a MeshPhongMaterial mesh with a co-located THREE.Points rendition sharing the same geometry. morphTargetInfluences[1] oscillates between 0 and 1 every 2 seconds, smoothly interpolating between the sphere and the Blob shape. First milestone to exercise the bridge's morph target path — nxjs's nx_webgl_compile_shader source-scan gains a third auto-promote marker (#define USE_MORPHTARGETS), so any Three.js material applied to a geometry with morph attributes routes through the raw-shader passthrough path (memory), running the auto-generated morph compose (morphtarget_pars_vertex + morphtarget_vertex chunks) verbatim on native GLES. Two PointLights (red + green) plus ambient.


GLTFLoader is not bundled in switch-web-browser; the upstream AnimatedMorphSphere.gltf + .bin are pre-parsed at build time (scripts/pack-morph-sphere.mjs) into a single morph-sphere.bin with a fixed header layout (positions + normals + 2 morph targets × positions + normals + indices) that the demo loads via fetch+arrayBuffer. GLTF deltas in this 218 KB asset survive the round-trip exactly — no resampling, no precision loss. MeshStandardMaterial (upstream) is substituted by MeshPhongMaterial({ color: 0xcccccc }) for visual parity through the bridge's lighting path; full PBR through raw_passthrough is deferred. Left stick orbits the camera. Re-uses the existing SwitchOrbitControls addon and inlines a tiny Timer equivalent (~20 LOC of getDelta()).
