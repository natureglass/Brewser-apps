Three.js r162 — Materials Wireframe

Port of webgl_materials_wireframe.html: two side-by-side WaltHeadLo meshes drawn as wireframes. Left uses MeshBasicMaterial({wireframe: true}) — Three.js's built-in line-mode wireframe, dispatched through the bridge's bridge_line_program. Right uses ShaderMaterial with a custom barycentric-coordinate fragment shader (fwidth() + gl_FrontFacing), dispatched through the new raw-shader passthrough path that bypasses the bridge's hardcoded-program swap.


Conformance: rendering/triangle — ported upstream Khronos test that draws a triangle with a custom fragment shader and verifies pixel output. Pins the raw-shader passthrough path: #pragma raw_passthrough in both shader sources, setupProgram + drawArrays, then readPixels to assert the rendered pixels match the shader's vec4(1,0,0,1) output. Was AVOID per nxjs-no-custom-fragment-shader before this milestone.

Left stick orbits the camera. Pan and zoom are disabled per upstream. The right-hand mesh exercises the new raw-shader passthrough path: its #pragma raw_passthrough-marked ShaderMaterial runs custom GLSL (fwidth(), gl_FrontFacing, smoothstep) on the GPU directly, bypassing the bridge's own hardcoded fragment shader. Look for P+N dispatch tags alongside the standard T+N / line-program tags in the bridge dbg row.
