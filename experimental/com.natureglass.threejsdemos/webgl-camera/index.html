Three.js r162 — Camera Perspective / Ortho

Port of three-r162/examples/webgl_camera.html: a camera rig hosts both a PerspectiveCamera and an OrthographicCamera that take turns rendering the left half of the canvas, while the right half shows a third "narrator" camera's view of the rig (visualized by a CameraHelper wireframe). First milestone to exercise (a) orthographic projection through the bridge's CPU-side transform_position3_depth, (b) renderer.setScissorTest + split-screen with two renderer.render() calls per frame, and (c) the new GL_POINTS draw path in nx_webgl_draw_arrays (each point is CPU-expanded to a 2-pixel screen-aligned quad through bridge_color_program).

Toggle the active rig camera with: B perspective · A orthographic (replaces upstream's O/P keys).


Conformance: rendering/gl-drawarrays — pins drawArrays primitive-mode gatekeeping including the new GL_POINTS path this milestone added (starfield).

The scene is rendered TWICE per frame: the left half (black background) shows what the currently-active rig camera sees of the wireframe sphere cluster and 10,000-point starfield. The right half (charcoal background) shows a wide narrator-camera view of the rig from outside, with the active camera's frustum drawn as a yellow wireframe via CameraHelper. Toggle ortho vs perspective on the active rig camera and watch the difference between the two projection types in the left half (perspective shows the starfield foreshortened toward a vanishing point; orthographic shows it as a uniformly-dense field).
