Three.js r162 — Geometry Shapes

Port of three-r162/examples/webgl_geometry_shapes.html: a gallery of 11 procedurally-generated 2D shapes (California outline, heart, smiley with holes, fish, spline, etc.), each rendered as 9 variants in z-stacks: textured flat ShapeGeometry, colored flat ShapeGeometry, colored ExtrudeGeometry (with bevel), 2 Line outlines, 2 Points sets. First milestone to exercise mapTransform mat3 uniform support in the bridge — Three.js's texture.repeat.set(0.008, 0.008) now scales UVs on the textured flat shapes so the UV grid tiles correctly rather than stretching the edge texel across the shape. Also exercises side: THREE.DoubleSide for the flat ShapeGeometry meshes.

Control: Right stick drag-rotates the group around the Y axis (replaces upstream's pointer drag).


Each shape is laid out in a z-stack of 9 variants (z = -175..125 in 50-unit steps). The textured flat ShapeGeometry uses the upstream UV-grid texture with RepeatWrapping and texture.repeat.set(0.008, 0.008) — that scaling is what mapTransform mat3 uniform support in the bridge enables. The 2 Points variants render at fixed ~2 px size regardless of PointsMaterial({ size: 4 }) because the bridge's draw_arrays_points doesn't honor the size uniform yet.
